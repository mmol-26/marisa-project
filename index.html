Overview

American Airlines is the largest airline in the world, operating the biggest fleet and serving more destinations than any other U.S. carrier. Yet despite its size, American consistently earns less profit than it's competitors, Delta and United. This project explores why that gap exists.

Using data from the Bureau of Transportation Statistics, consumer ratings, and financial statements, this analysis compares several key areas: on‑time performance, quarterly financial results, customer satisfaction scores, and overall passenger volume throughout 2025. Together, these metrics reveal how operational reliability, cost structure, and public perception contribute to American’s profitability challenges — and why Delta and United continue to outperform the world’s largest airline.

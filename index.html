Overview

This project analyzes the operational and customer experience performance of the three largest U.S. carriers: American Airlines (AA), Delta Air Lines (DL), and United Airlines (UA). Using real quarterly financial data, BTS operational metrics, and customer satisfaction benchmarks, this website highlights how each airline performs across cost structure, reliability, and customer perception.

As someone who works inside airline operations, I wanted this project to feel grounded in reality—not just numbers on a page. The aviation industry is complex, fast‑moving, and deeply interconnected. A single delay can ripple across the network. A shift in fuel prices can reshape an entire quarter. Customer satisfaction is influenced by everything from staffing to weather to aircraft routing.

This Overview page introduces the dataset and the purpose of the project. The Analysis and Conclusion pages will walk through the findings and explain what the differences between AA, DL, and UA mean in practice.
